# NotZero demo script

Target length: 2 minutes 45 seconds. Record at 1080p with browser zoom at 100 percent. Use English narration and no background music.

## 0:00 to 0:20. The problem

On screen: landing headline and the animated final-project transformation.

Narration:

> Ask a working graduate where they learned the tools they use every day, and many will say: on the job. A student then opens a job post, sees unfamiliar terms, and assumes years of education no longer count. NotZero shows that the job post looks unfamiliar, but their knowledge often does not.

## 0:20 to 0:40. The product

On screen: let the animation move from the 2022 project into foundations, bridges, and new learning. Briefly switch one multidisciplinary use case, then return to software development.

Narration:

> NotZero reads dated academic work and a bounded project, extracts only what the evidence supports, and compares that foundation with a reviewed current-practice pack. The mechanism can serve multiple fields, while this submitted vertical slice is deliberately validated for recent software graduates.

## 0:40 to 1:05. Evidence review

On screen: open Alex's prepared profile, continue through the target, build the bridge, and pause at the evidence checkpoint. Open one claim, then leave every claim included.

Narration:

> Alex is fictional, so judges can test the complete path without an account or private upload. Before comparison, the user reviews each extracted claim. They can reject an inference, and the comparison runs only on the included evidence without another upload.

## 1:05 to 1:35. Decision first

On screen: select Compare included evidence, skip or let the reveal settle, then hold on the first viewport. Point to the headline, shortest bridge, and first action.

Narration:

> The result starts with a decision, not a wall of model output. Alex's evidence already connects to reviewed role requirements. His shortest bridge is containerization, and the first action reuses the configuration, dependencies, ports, and startup sequence already present in his project.

## 1:35 to 2:05. Trust and learning delta

On screen: open and close one numbered citation receipt. Open the then-and-now walkthrough. Show one next step's Why this step control.

Narration:

> Every conclusion resolves to an exact submitted excerpt or a dated market source. The walkthrough separates what transfers from what remains genuinely new, and labels unexecuted modernization work as illustrative. Each next step names what it reuses, what must be learned, why teams use it, and what artifact proves the bridge.

## 2:05 to 2:25. A result worth keeping

On screen: change the supported priority once, then use Save bridge card. Open the print dialog with Download report and cancel it after the preview appears.

Narration:

> The user can change the supported priority without uploading evidence again. They can save a privacy-safe summary card or a full report generated from the same validated payload shown on screen.

## 2:25 to 2:45. Codex and GPT-5.6

On screen: finish on the Method page or a simple repository architecture view prepared before recording.

Narration:

> Codex helped build and test the complete product across the interface, typed contracts, evidence pipeline, accessibility, security controls, and deployment. GPT-5.6 powers three schema-constrained server stages: evidence extraction, the Knowledge Bridge comparison, and the guided program with its vocabulary, code counterparts, and curriculum. Independent validation rejects invented claim IDs, excerpts, locators, dates, and market counts before a result reaches the user.

## Recording checklist

- Keep the video at or below 2 minutes 55 seconds after editing.
- Show the working product continuously. Avoid long repository tours.
- Make the citation panel opening and closing visible.
- Keep the first decision viewport on screen long enough to read.
- Confirm the PDF print preview appears and the PNG downloads.
- Mention Codex and GPT-5.6 by name in the narration.
- Upload to YouTube as public, then verify it while logged out.

The current official requirements specify a public YouTube video of three minutes or less, a working demo, audio narration, and coverage of the product, Codex, and GPT-5.6. Recheck the [Official Rules](https://openai.devpost.com/rules) and [FAQ](https://openai.devpost.com/details/faqs) immediately before recording and submitting.
